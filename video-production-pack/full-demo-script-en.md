# Full Demo Script - English

Recommended length: 2 minutes 30 seconds to 3 minutes.

Tone: professional, product-oriented, concise.

## 0:00-0:15 Opening

Growth Strategy Copilot is an AI-assisted growth diagnosis workspace for subscription-based businesses. It helps teams move from business context and operating data to metric diagnosis, user segmentation, strategy prioritization, experiment planning, and executive reporting.

## 0:15-0:35 Product Motivation

Growth analysis often follows a repeated workflow. Teams define the business problem, check core metrics, identify bottlenecks, segment users, prioritize strategies, design tests, and turn findings into a management-ready narrative. Growth Strategy Copilot structures that workflow into a guided product experience.

## 0:35-0:55 Demo Setup

This demo uses synthetic data and mock AI. The goal is to demonstrate the workflow, not to claim real business inference. The sample case is a fitness subscription platform, with simulated user behavior across activation, engagement, payment, churn risk, and campaign signals.

## 0:55-1:25 Diagnosis

The Diagnosis page starts with deterministic metric calculation. The system computes activation rate, paid conversion, average completion, 30-day active user rate, observed churn, and ARPU. Instead of asking AI to freely decide the business issue, the product applies visible rules and thresholds. Each finding includes evidence, interpretation, rule confidence, and methodology notes, so the diagnosis remains traceable.

## 1:25-1:50 Segmentation

Next, the product turns aggregate metrics into user segments. Users are grouped by behavior, payment status, activity recency, and price sensitivity. This creates action-oriented segments such as new users, high-potential unpaid users, high-value users, churn-risk users, and price-sensitive users. The goal is to connect the diagnosis to specific user groups, not just broad averages.

## 1:50-2:15 Strategy

The Strategy page converts findings into scored recommendations. Strategy templates are not shown as a fixed generic list. They are prioritized using bottleneck severity, affected segment size, primary goal alignment, evidence strength, and implementation effort. This produces P0, P1, and P2 recommendations, with a visible rationale behind each priority decision.

## 2:15-2:35 Experiments

P0 and P1 strategies are then translated into A/B test plans. Each experiment includes the hypothesis, target users, control group, treatment group, primary metrics, secondary metrics, randomization unit, feasibility note, and statistical decision rule. This keeps strategy connected to validation.

## 2:35-2:55 Report

Finally, the Report page packages the same analysis into an executive summary, detailed report, and presentation outline. The product also supports English and Chinese localization, so the same workflow can be presented in different user contexts without changing the underlying data logic.

## 2:55-3:00 Closing

Growth Strategy Copilot shows how AI can support growth analysis while keeping the core decisions rules-first, structured, and reviewable.
