# Video Demo Guide

## 1. Video Purpose

Create a polished 2-3 minute product demo that shows Growth Strategy Copilot as an AI-assisted growth diagnosis workspace, not a generic chatbot or static report generator.

The video should demonstrate how the product connects business context, synthetic subscription data, metric diagnosis, user segmentation, strategy scoring, experiment planning, and executive reporting into one structured workflow.

## 2. Target Audience

- AI product managers and product strategy reviewers.
- Growth, subscription, and platform business stakeholders.
- Portfolio viewers who want to understand the product logic quickly.
- Technical reviewers who care about the separation between deterministic rules and AI-assisted narrative.

## 3. Recommended Video Length

- Primary portfolio demo: 2-3 minutes.
- Short social preview: 60 seconds.
- Keep the main video focused on the workflow, evidence chain, and product reasoning rather than implementation details.

## 4. Recording Setup

- Record the production URL: <https://growth-strategy-copilot.vercel.app>
- Use Chrome, Edge, or another modern desktop browser.
- Use English mode first, then briefly show the English / Chinese switch near the end.
- Close unrelated tabs and applications.
- Hide bookmarks bar if it creates visual clutter.
- Use a neutral desktop background.
- Use a clear microphone if recording live narration; otherwise record voiceover after the screen capture.

## 5. Screen Size Recommendation

- Preferred: 1440px desktop width.
- Also suitable: 1280px width for a 13-inch laptop-style recording.
- Browser zoom: 90% or 100%.
- Avoid recording below tablet width for the main video unless the purpose is specifically responsive QA.

## 6. Step-by-Step Recording Flow

1. Open the production URL.
2. Click **Reset Demo** if there is existing local state.
3. Click **Start Demo with Fitness Sample**.
4. Show the Dashboard workflow and sample-data limitation.
5. Move through Data Upload and run diagnosis if needed.
6. Show Diagnosis, emphasizing metric definitions and bottleneck evidence.
7. Show Segmentation, emphasizing behavior-based target groups.
8. Show Strategy, emphasizing scoring logic and P0 strategy focus.
9. Show Experiments, emphasizing A/B test structure and guardrails.
10. Show Report, emphasizing executive summary, detailed report, presentation outline, and bilingual output.
11. End on the production URL or Dashboard.

## 7. Full 3-Minute English Voiceover Script

### 0:00-0:20 Product Opening

"This is Growth Strategy Copilot, an AI-assisted business analysis workspace for subscription-based growth teams. Instead of starting from an open-ended chatbot prompt, the product guides the user through a structured workflow: business setup, data upload, metric diagnosis, user segmentation, strategy prioritization, experiment design, and executive reporting."

### 0:20-0:40 Why I Built This

"The idea came from repeated workflows I saw in growth analysis projects, especially subscription business cases. Analysts often need to connect user behavior, business context, and operating metrics into actionable strategy recommendations. Growth Strategy Copilot explores how that workflow can become a more structured AI-assisted product."

### 0:40-1:10 Dashboard and Demo Setup

"For this demo, I am using a synthetic fitness subscription dataset. The sample data is designed to demonstrate the product workflow; it should not be treated as evidence about a real company or used for production-level causal inference. I can start the demo with one click, which loads the recommended business context and sample data so the analysis path is ready to explore."

### 1:10-1:45 Diagnosis

"On the Diagnosis page, the system calculates defined subscription metrics such as activation, paid conversion, 30-day active user rate, churn, completion rate, and ARPU. The main bottleneck is not generated freely by AI. It is detected through visible rule-based logic, with evidence, metric definitions, confidence level, and root cause hypotheses. The goal is decision support with traceable reasoning."

### 1:45-2:10 Segmentation and Strategy

"Next, the product turns aggregate signals into user segments such as new users, high-potential unpaid users, churn-risk users, and price-sensitive users. Strategy recommendations are then scored using a transparent formula that considers bottleneck severity, affected segment size, goal alignment, evidence strength, and implementation effort. This creates a priority ranking instead of a fixed list of generic suggestions."

### 2:10-2:35 Experiments

"P0 and P1 strategies are converted into A/B test plans. Each experiment includes the hypothesis, target users, control and treatment groups, primary and secondary metrics, randomization unit, feasibility notes, and a statistical decision rule. This keeps the strategy connected to validation rather than stopping at a recommendation."

### 2:35-2:55 Report

"The Report page packages the same analysis into an executive summary, a detailed report, and a presentation outline. The bilingual language switch also localizes the UI and report output for English or Chinese product demos, while keeping the underlying data schema and calculations consistent."

### 2:55-3:00 Closing

"The current version is a demo-ready MVP using synthetic data and mock AI output. Its value is showing how AI can support business analysis while keeping the core analytical decisions rule-driven, transparent, and reviewable."

## 10. On-Screen Captions

- AI-assisted growth diagnosis workspace
- Rules first, AI second
- Metric-driven bottleneck detection
- Synthetic data for workflow demonstration
- User segmentation for targeted strategy
- Transparent strategy prioritization
- From strategy to A/B test design
- Executive-ready reporting
- English / Chinese localization
- Mock AI output, production-ready integration boundary

## 11. What to Emphasize

- The product is a structured workspace, not a chatbot.
- Metrics and diagnosis are rule-driven and auditable.
- Root causes are presented as hypotheses, not confirmed causal claims.
- Strategy priority is calculated from transparent scoring inputs.
- Experiments connect recommendations to validation.
- Reports reuse the same analysis rather than inventing new conclusions.
- The demo is deployable and shareable through the production Vercel URL.

## 12. Avoid These Claims

1. Do not claim this analyzes a real company.
2. Do not claim the synthetic dataset proves real business outcomes.
3. Do not claim AI independently makes business decisions.
4. Do not claim experiment sizing is production-ready.
5. Do not claim the current mock AI is a live LLM integration.
6. Do not imply the tool has a cloud database or cross-device state sync.
7. Do not present root cause hypotheses as confirmed causal explanations.
8. Do not claim acquisition trend or month-two retention is proven by the current point-in-time sample.

## 13. What Not to Overclaim

- Say "supports decision-making" rather than "solves growth problems."
- Say "synthetic sample data shows the workflow" rather than "the data proves the business issue."
- Say "mock AI output" rather than "live AI analysis."
- Say "experiment planning" rather than "validated business impact."
- Say "browser-local demo state" rather than "cloud-synced workspace."

## 14. On-Screen Actions

- Keep cursor movements slow and intentional.
- Pause on Diagnosis, Strategy, Experiments, and Report long enough for viewers to read.
- Use short scrolls rather than fast page jumps.
- Collapse long cards if the screen becomes crowded.
- When showing Chinese mode, switch once and briefly pause on the localized report.
- Return to English before ending if the target audience is primarily English-facing.

## 15. Final Checklist Before Recording

- Production URL opens successfully.
- Demo state is reset.
- English mode is selected at the start.
- Browser zoom is 90% or 100%.
- Bookmarks bar and unrelated tabs are hidden.
- Microphone is working or voiceover plan is ready.
- Start Demo loads the fitness sample correctly.
- Diagnosis, Strategy, Experiments, and Report pages are accessible.
- Sample data limitation is visible or mentioned.
- No browser console errors are visible.
- Recording resolution is suitable for 1080p export.

## 16. Chinese Voiceover Script

### 0:00-0:20 产品开场

“这是 Growth Strategy Copilot，一个面向订阅制业务的 AI 辅助增长诊断工作台。它不是普通聊天机器人，也不是一次性报告生成器，而是把业务背景、运营数据、指标诊断、用户分层、策略优先级、实验设计和管理层汇报串联成一条结构化工作流。”

### 0:20-0:40 项目动机

“这个项目的灵感来自我在商业分析项目中的经历，尤其是健身订阅平台的增长策略分析。很多增长分析工作都有相似流程：先定义业务问题，再检查指标，识别瓶颈，划分用户群，制定策略，设计实验，最后把分析转化成可汇报的结论。”

### 0:40-1:10 Dashboard 与样例设置

“这里我使用的是一份合成的健身订阅样例数据。它的作用是展示完整分析流程，不代表真实公司数据，也不能用于生产级因果推断或实验样本量估算。点击 Start Demo 后，系统会自动加载推荐业务背景和样例数据，方便快速进入演示。”

### 1:10-1:45 指标诊断

“在 Diagnosis 页面，系统会计算激活率、付费转化、30 天活跃用户率、流失率、课程完成率和 ARPU 等指标。主要瓶颈不是由 AI 自由生成，而是由规则引擎基于阈值和数据证据识别。每个结论都会显示指标依据、业务解释、证据类型和规则置信度。”

### 1:45-2:10 用户分层与策略

“接下来，系统会把整体指标信号转化成用户分层，例如新用户、高潜未付费用户、流失风险用户和价格敏感用户。策略不是固定排序，而是根据瓶颈严重程度、受影响用户规模、业务目标匹配度、证据强度和执行难度进行评分，从而形成 P0、P1、P2 优先级。”

### 2:10-2:35 实验设计

“对于 P0 和 P1 策略，系统会生成 A/B 测试方案，包括实验假设、目标用户、对照组、实验组、核心指标、辅助指标、随机分组单位、可行性说明和统计决策规则。这样策略不会停留在建议层面，而是进入可验证的增长实验。”

### 2:35-2:55 汇报输出与本地化

“Report 页面会把前面的分析结果整理成管理层摘要、详细分析报告和 PPT 大纲。产品也支持英文和中文切换，导航、指标说明、策略卡片、实验方案和报告输出都会根据语言选择进行展示。”

### 2:55-3:00 结尾

“当前版本是一个使用合成数据和 mock AI 输出的 MVP。它的重点不是证明某个真实业务结论，而是展示如何让 AI 在规则驱动、证据可追溯的框架下辅助商业分析和策略表达。”
